            <h2>Firefighter Career Profile</h2>


            <h3>Description</h3>
            <p>Firefighters respond to fire alarms, rescue individuals, administer first aid, and educate the public on fire safety. They also inspect buildings for fire hazards and participate in training drills.</p>


            <h3>Working Conditions</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Hours:</strong> Shift work, typically 24-hour shifts followed by 48 hours off.</li>
                <li><strong>Environment:</strong> High-risk situations, exposure to fire, smoke, and hazardous materials.</li>
            </ul>


            <h3>Training Required</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Education:</strong> NFPA 1001 Level 1 & 2 and NFPA 1072 certifications.</li>
                <li><strong>Cost:</strong> $10,000 to $15,000 for private programs; municipal departments may provide training.</li>
            </ul>


            <h3>Future Outlook</h3>
            <p>Steady demand for firefighters is expected, driven by urban development and climate change. Advancements in technology, such as drones and thermal imaging cameras, are also enhancing the profession.</p>


            <h3>Salary</h3>
            <p>Average salary in Ottawa: $81,185 annually, with a range of $55,855 to $99,127.</p>


            <h3>Advantages</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Job Satisfaction:</strong> Helping others, community involvement, teamwork.</li>
                <li><strong>Benefits:</strong> Comprehensive health benefits, pension plans, and job security.</li>
            </ul>


            <h3>Disadvantages</h3>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>Risks:</strong> Exposure to dangerous situations, potential for injury.</li>
                <li><strong>Stress:</strong> High-stress environment, emotional toll from emergency situations.</li>
            </ul>


            <h3>Summary</h3>
            <p>Combining a background in construction with firefighting can be advantageous, as skills such as physical stamina, understanding of building structures, and hands-on tool experience are transferable and beneficial in firefighting scenarios. My personal values align with the firefighter's mission to protect and serve the community, making this career a strong fit for my personality and goals.</p>
